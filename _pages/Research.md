My research combines globally distributed measurements from the Surface Particulate Matter Network (SPARTAN) with chemical transport modeling (GEOS-Chem) to improve the understanding of aerosol composition, evaluate emission inventories, and develop measurement techniques for global air quality and health applications.

**Offline Aerosol Mass Spectrometry Methodology for Organic Aerosol Characterization** (<a href="https://doi.org/10.5194/egusphere-2026-2663" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ren et al., <i>Atmos. Meas. Tech.</i>, 2026</a>)

<p><img src="{{ base_path }}/images/2026_AMT_Ren.png" class="pub-figure-right" alt="Ren et al., Atmospheric Measurement Techniques, 2026 figure" />Global organic aerosol (OA) measurements remain sparse and unevenly distributed. While Aerosol Mass Spectrometry (AMS) is widely used for real-time characterization of OA composition, its cost, complexity, and logistical requirements limit long-term, multi-site deployment, particularly at the global scale. This study develops and evaluates an offline AMS methodology to characterize OA in particulate matter collected on PTFE filters. The method requires only small extract volumes, achieves high nebulization efficiency and low detection limits, and is validated against co-located online AMS measurements. By extending AMS analysis to archived filter samples and monitoring networks worldwide, this methodology enables construction of a long-term, globally consistent OA dataset.</p>

**Black Carbon Emissions Generally Underestimated in the Global South** (<a href="https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-62468-5" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ren et al., <i>Nat. Comm.</i>, 2025</a>)

<p><img src="{{ base_path }}/images/2025_NatComm_Ren.png" class="pub-figure-right" alt="Ren et al., Nature Communications, 2025 figure" />Large uncertainties in black carbon (BC) emissions persist because long-term observations remain sparse across much of the world. Using globally distributed SPARTAN measurements together with high-resolution GEOS-Chem simulations, this study evaluates global BC emission inventories. We find high concsistency across primarily developed regions in the northern midlatitudes, but pronounced discrepancies in the Global South. These findings motivate renewed attention to the challenging task of characterizing BC emissions from low- and middle-income countries.</p>

**Bioaccessibility and Health Risks of Heavy Metals in Particulate Matter** (<a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.scitotenv.2024.174303" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ren et al., <i>Sci. Total Environ.</i>, 2024</a>; <a href="https://doi.org/10.1016/j.chemosphere.2021.130312" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Ren et al., <i>Chemosphere</i>, 2021</a>)

<p><img src="{{ base_path }}/images/2024_STOTEN_Ren.jpg" class="pub-figure-right" alt="Ren et al., Science of the Total Environment, 2024 figure" />Health risks associated with particulate matter depend not only on the abundance of toxic metals but also on their bioaccessibility after inhalation. This study investigates the concentrations, bioaccessibility, and emission sources of PM-bound heavy metals across twelve Chinese cities and evaluates their associated health risks. Our results reveal substantial variability among individual metals and emission sources, highlighting the importance of incorporating bioaccessibility into exposure assessment and supporting more effective, source-oriented air pollution control strategies.</p>
